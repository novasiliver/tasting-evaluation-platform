3. The 5-Step Certification Process

Our evaluation process typically takes 4-6 weeks from submission to certification issuance.

1Submit Product

Timeline: Days 1-3

Complete our online submission form with product details, ingredient lists, and origin documentation. Ship 3 sample units to our evaluation facility.

  • Secure online portal for data submission
  • Category specialist assigned to your product
  • Pre-screening for eligibility

2Blind Sensory Panel

Timeline: Days 4-10

Upon arrival, all branding is removed and samples are anonymized. Expert tasters evaluate your product in ISO-standard sensory booths without knowing the producer identity.

  • 5-7 certified sensory professionals per panel
  • Controlled environment (temperature, lighting, humidity)
  • Independent scoring‚Äîno panelist sees others' evaluations

3Technical Lab Analysis

Timeline: Days 11-21

Parallel laboratory testing verifies composition, purity, and authenticity. Tests may include chemical profiling, microbiological screening, and geographic markers.

  • Conducted by accredited third-party labs (ISO 17025 certified)
  • Results must align with sensory findings
  • Ensures product integrity and safety

4Scoring & Detailed Feedback

Timeline: Days 22-25

Data from sensory panels and lab tests are compiled into a comprehensive report with your final score (out of 100 points) and detailed feedback.

  • Scores calculated as trimmed mean (removes outliers)
  • Breakdown by sensory attributes (appearance, aroma, flavor, texture, finish)
  • Actionable insights for product improvement

5Earn Certification Seal

Timeline: Day 28

Products scoring 85+ out of 100 earn the coveted Gold Seal. You receive digital seal files, a physical certificate, and listing in our public directory.

  • License to use seal on packaging and marketing
  • Unique verification number (format: TC-YYYY-XXXX)
  • 3-year validity with optional renewal

4. Understanding the 100-Point Scoring System

Our scoring methodology evaluates products across five sensory dimensions. Scores are benchmarked against global standards and category-specific excellence criteria.

Attribute Weight What's Evaluated
Appearance 10% Visual clarity, color intensity, consistency, presentation appeal
Aroma/Bouquet 25% Aromatic intensity, complexity, typicity, absence of off-notes
Flavor/Taste 35% Taste balance, flavor depth, complexity, finish quality
Mouthfeel/Texture 15% Body, viscosity, smoothness, textural harmony
Overall Harmony 15% Integration of attributes, finish length, overall impression

Scoring is Transparent & Data-Driven

  • Final scores = trimmed mean of all panel member scores
  • Statistical outliers removed to minimize individual bias
  • Benchmarked against category excellence standards
  • Results reproducible and defensible

6. How to Prepare for Submission

Pre-Submission Checklist

  • Product is shelf-stable or can survive 5-7 day shipping
  • All required food safety certifications are current
  • Ingredient list and nutrition facts are finalized
  • Packaging clearly identifies product name and producer
  • You have 3 identical sample units available
  • Product has been on market for at least 6 months (preferred)

Required Documentation

  1. Producer Profile: Company name, contact, production location
  2. Product Details: Name, category, ingredient list, production method
  3. Origin Documentation: Source verification for key ingredients
  4. Food Safety Certificates: Current health department approval, if applicable
  5. Lab Reports: Any existing quality tests (optional but helpful)

Pro Tips for Success

  • Ship Fresh: Send samples at peak quality‚Äîavoid products near expiration
  • Proper Packaging: Ensure samples arrive intact (use protective packaging)
  • Consistency Matters: Submit products representative of your typical batch quality
  • Choose the Right Category: Accurate categorization ensures fair benchmarking

Sample Shipping Guidelines

  • Ship via trackable carrier (FedEx, UPS, DHL)
  • Include insulation for temperature-sensitive products
  • Label clearly: "ATTN: Tastecert Evaluation Lab"
  • Avoid shipping on Fridays (to prevent weekend delays)

9. Frequently Asked Questions

How is Tastecert different from other awards?

Most food awards are pay-to-play marketing schemes where every entrant receives some level of recognition. Tastecert uses blind sensory evaluation and data-driven scoring. Products scoring below 65/100 do not receive certification‚Äîwe maintain rigorous standards.

What if my product doesn't achieve certification?

You still receive a comprehensive evaluation report with detailed feedback. Many producers use this information to refine their products and resubmit 6-12 months later with excellent results.

Is there a refund policy?

Evaluation fees are non-refundable once samples enter the testing process, as expert time and lab resources are committed. However, if we determine your product is ineligible before evaluation begins, a full refund is issued.

Can I use the seal on packaging immediately?

Yes, upon certification issuance you receive an immediate license to use the seal on all packaging, marketing, and digital assets. We provide high-resolution files in multiple formats.

Who are the sensory panel members?

Our panels consist of certified professionals with credentials such as: Certified Sensory Analyst, Q Grader (coffee), WSET Level 3+ (wine/spirits), Master Sommelier, or equivalent. All panelists undergo quarterly calibration testing.

What categories do you certify?

We evaluate most food and beverage categories including: olive oil, coffee, chocolate, honey, spirits, wine, specialty sauces, artisan cheeses, and more. Contact us if unsure about your product's eligibility.

How long is certification valid?

Certifications are valid for 3 years. We recommend recertification before expiration to maintain continuous seal usage rights. Many producers recertify with product improvements to achieve higher scores.

What happens if my product formulation changes?

Minor changes (e.g., new packaging) don't require recertification. Significant changes to ingredients or production methods require resubmission as these affect sensory characteristics.
